Composition Target

6–8 Healers — AoE sustain wins team fights · 4 Tanks — frontline anchor, CC immunity · 1 Duelist — Nameless Sword preferred, pre-assigned · Rest: DPS — prioritise Nameless Sword and Twin Blade

Critical

Always pre-assign all 3 Performer slots before the match. If any slot is empty and that player is absent, the game auto-picks a random guild member — this could be your healer against their best 1v1 player.

What Are Flex Groups?

Flex Groups are your two 5-player roaming squads that take the top and bottom jungle routes while the Main Force holds mid. They have two jobs — and both are essential:

1
Farm jungle mobs every 5 minutes Mob camps respawn on a timer. Each kill earns Fun Coins — the resource the Commander spends on team buffs. No Flex farming = Commander has nothing to spend = team fights in the dark.
2
Hunt enemy healers in side lanes Enemy healers are the highest priority target. Flex groups pressure them from off-angles, forcing healers to split from the main group or die away from protection.
3
Collapse mid on Commander's call When the Commander calls PUSH MID or GOOSE — Flex Groups immediately stop what they're doing and converge on the main fight. Don't farm during a decisive push.
Best weapons for Flex

Twin Blade — high mobility, applies Anti-Heal (critical for Nullify Healing step in the team fight combo) · Strategic Sword — strong poke and disengage for farming safely. Avoid putting tanks or Nameless Swords in Flex — they're needed on the frontline.

Playing With a Small Roster

Fewer than 30 players? Adjust the split — never spread thin enough to lose every lane.

Players OnlineFormationNotes
25–30 20 Main Force · 1 Flex Group (5) · drop 1 Flex Send the single Flex Group to whichever jungle side has more mobs or weaker enemy coverage
20–24 All players → Main Force mid · no Flex split Stack numbers where they matter most — mid lane and towers. Farm is sacrificed, so Commander must spend coins wisely on timed pushes
15–19 Full stack mid · turtle strategy Hold your own towers. Defend and chip. Commander saves all coins for City Protection and Last Stand. Win on tower HP tiebreak if you can't push tree.
Under 15 Defend one tower · protect it with everything You will likely lose on points — the goal is to minimise the defeat and preserve rank standing
Always tell the Commander your count

Before the match starts, confirm in chat how many players are actually online. The Commander's entire strategy — split assignments, coin spending plan, whether to attempt Flex routes — depends on knowing the real headcount going in.

Why Heartless Script?

CC is constant in 30v30. Being stunned, silenced, or frozen is when you take the most burst damage. 20% reduction during those windows keeps you alive long enough to counterattack. Alternatives only if you have zero CC pressure.

The 6-step combo. If every step lands, the fight ends in seconds.

1
Tank Pull Vacuum/pull ultimate drags enemies into a tight cluster
2

3

5
Lion's Roar Silences the trapped, heal-blocked group — area silence + damage reduction
6
Everyone Bursts AoE abilities and EX skills into the clustered, silenced, anti-healed group

After winning a fight

Immediately attack the nearest tower or Goose while the enemy respawns. Don't stand around. Always force engagements near enemy towers — every AoE that hits enemies near a tower also chips the tower.

Common Mistakes
What NOT To Do

Mistakes That Lose Matches

✕Do NOT rush straight down mid only — no Flex Groups = no fun coins = Commander has nothing to spend
✕Do NOT split into 3 lanes with fewer than 30 players — you get bullied in every lane; focus mid first
✕Do NOT attack the enemy Goose before at least 1 tower is down — it takes zero damage until then
✕Do NOT send an unprepared player into the halftime duel — a healer vs their best 1v1 player ends the match

Mistakes That Waste Resources

✕Do NOT use Refight (2000 coins) before the final decisive push — it's your most expensive card
✕Do NOT activate Hair Pulling unless the whole team is grouped and EX skills are ready — 10s window wasted on 5 people
✕Do NOT try to interrupt Lion's Roar mid-cast — Fortitude blocks all CC; you will waste your stagger on nothing

Mistakes That Get People Killed

✕Do NOT fight away from towers — every AoE near a tower chips the structure for free
✕Do NOT chase kills past the enemy frontline — no healer coverage behind their line = death
✕Do NOT overextend in the final 10 minutes — respawn timers are very long; one bad push loses the game
Enemy Skills
Countering Enemy Skills
Lion's Roar (The Bell) — Area Silence + Damage Reduction
It CAN be interrupted — use Cloud Steps

Lion's Roar grants Fortitude (anti-stagger) while casting, so normal CC fails. However, Cloud Steps targets Unstable Poise from above — its aerial stomp triggers a Weak Point Break that cuts through Fortitude and cancels the cast entirely. Assign at least one Cloud Steps user per fight for this purpose.

PriorityCounter
1stCloud Steps aerial stomp — dive from above to trigger Weak Point Break and cancel the cast. Best counter available.
2ndSpread formation — don't cluster. Silence only triggers on targets hit 5+ times out of 14 hits. Spread = fewer hits per player
3rdDodge on bell slam (watch for the slam animation) — roll away before hit 5 lands on you
4thUse the knockback — the bell pushes you away; let it launch you out of hit range rather than fighting it
5thNameless Sword Z-key — raid shield absorbs the Qi damage from the roar for nearby allies
6thSpear Guardian Field (GvG ultimate) — CC immunity circle; allies inside cannot be Silenced

Core Rules
Key Principles
1 — Coordination beats individual skill

Every time. A coordinated average team beats a disorganised great team in 30v30.

2 — Protect your healers

If they die, your GvG run is effectively over. Target priority 1 is enemy healers — they know yours are priority too.

3 — Commander buffs flip fights

Timing is everything. Hair Pulling at the wrong moment wastes 300 coins and potentially the match.

4 — Use friendly matches

Unlimited friendly matches exist. Use them to drill the team fight combo, practice roles, and test the callout system before ranked Saturdays.
